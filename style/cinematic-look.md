# The "Professionally Shot" Look Bible

The counterpart to the iPhone look. Same clone, same face and voice — but rendered like a
**commercial / short film / high-end brand piece**. Where the iPhone look wins on *candid
and real*, this look wins on *intentional, controlled, and expensive-feeling*. Use it for
brand content, hero pieces, and anything that should feel produced.

This is where **Higgsfield** earns its place: its cinematic camera-motion presets are the
easiest way to get deliberate, motivated moves.

---

## The 6 pillars of "professionally shot"

### 1. The lens & camera (reads as "cinema")
- Shot on a **cinema camera** (ARRI Alexa / RED look), **anamorphic** or fast prime lens
- **Shallow depth of field** — creamy background bokeh, subject sharply separated
- Focal-length intent: 35/50/85mm equivalents; **subtle lens breathing**, anamorphic flares
- **16:9 or 2.39:1** cinematic aspect (or a clean 9:16 crop that still feels composed)

### 2. Camera movement (motivated, not floaty) — the Higgsfield zone
- **Deliberate moves:** slow dolly-in, crane up/down, orbit, push-in on a line, FPV whip
- **Gimbal-smooth** is *good* here (opposite of the iPhone look)
- Rack focus between subject and foreground/background
- Every move has a reason — reveal, emphasis, momentum

### 3. Light — designed, shaped, controlled
- **Three-point / motivated lighting:** key + fill + rim/hair light separating subject from background
- **Practical lights** in frame (neon, lamps, window) for depth
- **Volumetric light / haze** — god rays, atmosphere, soft falloff
- Big soft key for beauty, or hard directional for drama — *chosen*, not available
- Color contrast: warm subject vs cool background (classic teal-and-orange, used tastefully)

### 4. Composition & production design
- **Intentional framing:** rule of thirds, leading lines, negative space, symmetry when wanted
- **Depth in layers:** clear foreground / midground / background
- **Set dressing:** the space looks styled, not accidental
- Wardrobe deliberately styled and lit

### 5. Color grade (the "expensive" feel)
- **Filmic color grade:** rich but controlled saturation, gentle highlight roll-off, filmic contrast curve
- **Film emulation** (Kodak 2383 / Portra vibe), clean shadows, cinematic LUT
- Consistent palette across the piece

### 6. Skin — still real, just beautifully lit
- **Keep real skin texture** (pores, natural detail) — high-end work is *not* airbrushed-plastic
- Flatter with **light and lens**, not by smoothing. Beauty lighting > beauty retouch.
- This keeps it hyper-real even while looking produced — the whole point.

## Drop-in prompt phrases

**Camera / format**
> shot on a cinema camera, anamorphic prime lens, shallow depth of field with creamy bokeh, subject sharply separated from background, cinematic 2.39:1 framing, subtle anamorphic lens flare

**Motion**
> smooth deliberate camera movement — slow dolly-in (OR crane up OR orbit OR push-in), motivated and controlled, gimbal-stable, rack focus

**Light**
> designed cinematic lighting, soft key with rim light separating subject, practical lights in frame, volumetric haze and god rays, warm-cool color contrast

**Grade / finish**
> filmic color grade, film emulation, rich controlled color, gentle highlight roll-off, cinematic contrast, high production value, commercial quality

**Realism (keep it)**
> hyper-realistic, natural skin texture with visible pores, not airbrushed, flattered by lighting not retouching, photoreal

**Negative / avoid**
> avoid: flat lighting, amateur snapshot, phone-camera look, harsh on-camera flash, muddy color, plastic smooth skin, over-sharpened, cluttered background, blown-out video look

## Higgsfield camera-move cheat sheet

| Move | Feeling | Use for |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Slow push-in** | intimacy, importance | a key line, an emotional beat |
| **Crane up / reveal** | scale, arrival | establishing, "hero" reveal |
| **Orbit / 360** | showcase, product | showing the subject/outfit off |
| **FPV / drone whip** | energy, momentum | fast openers, transitions |
| **Bullet-time** | spectacle | a stylized freeze moment |
| **Dolly out** | release, loneliness, "the end" | closing shots |

## iPhone vs Professional — pick per piece

| | iPhone-real | Professionally shot |
|---|---|---|
| **Feeling** | candid, personal, "a friend filmed this" | intentional, premium, brand |
| **DOF** | deep, everything in focus | shallow, creamy bokeh |
| **Light** | available/natural | designed/controlled |
| **Motion** | handheld, shaky | gimbal-smooth, motivated |
| **Grade** | phone-HDR, minimal | filmic LUT, graded |
| **Best for** | day-in-life, stories, authenticity | hero pieces, brand, launches |
| **Key tools** | Veo/Sora/Kling + finishing | Higgsfield + Veo/Sora + grade |

Both use the **same clone**. Mixing them in a feed (mostly iPhone-real, occasional
polished hero piece) is what makes a creator feel *both* authentic and premium.

## The finishing pass (professional)
1. **Grade** with a filmic LUT; balance skin tones; set the palette.
2. **Add subtle film grain** (yes, even here — it kills the "digital AI" flatness).
3. **Sound design:** clean VO + scored music + designed SFX + room tone.
4. **Upscale** finals through **Topaz Video AI** for real-camera micro-detail.
5. Keep an **AI/synthetic disclosure** label.
